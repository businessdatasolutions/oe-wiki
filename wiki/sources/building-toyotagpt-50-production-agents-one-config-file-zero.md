## Summary

Ravi Chandu Ummadisetti, Head of Agent AI and Product Research at Toyota, and Kordel France, Head of AI Engineering at Toyota, detail the development of ToyotaGPT, an enterprise AI platform that significantly accelerates the deployment of AI agents. Confronted with widespread, uncoordinated AI initiatives across Toyota in 2023, their team built a standardized platform from scratch to prevent duplication and ensure security. This platform reduced the time and resources required to deliver a RAG (Retrieval Augmented Generation) app from six months and six engineers to just four days and one engineer, now supporting over 50 agents in production.

The ToyotaGPT architecture is characterized by dynamic, LangGraph-based graph generation, automatically synthesized skills from unstructured data, and an MCP-compatible tool layer fortified by TMNA cybersecurity from its inception. The system addresses Toyota's complex data reality, handling diverse formats like PDFs, CAD files, and scanned manuals from the 90s, across multiple languages, through layout-aware parsing and OCR. Examples of agents built on this platform include GearPull, which moved from a hackathon idea to saving millions in manufacturing by instantly providing solutions to production line issues, R&D GPT, which compresses years of paint research into seconds by leveraging institutional knowledge, and KadyaGPT, an AI design agent embedded directly into the designer's workflow for real-time best practice queries and pattern identification.

Kordel France draws compelling parallels between the Toyota Production System (TPS) and the LangChain ecosystem. He maps the TPS "Andon board" to LangSmith's real-time observability for monitoring agent performance, "Kaizen" (continuous improvement) to LangChain's constant evolution and agents' self-improvement, and "Jidoka" (automation with a human touch) to LangGraph's human-in-the-loop design, which balances automation with critical human oversight. Furthermore, "Genchi Gembutsu" (go to the source) is likened to LangSmith's trace-level debugging, enabling engineers to pinpoint root causes of issues efficiently.

**Why it matters for Operations Management:** This presentation offers a practical case study in rapidly deploying AI solutions within a large enterprise, directly impacting productivity and process design. ToyotaGPT's ability to drastically reduce development cycles and engineer count for AI agents highlights advancements in technology adoption and efficient resource allocation. The emphasis on standardized platforms and security addresses critical concerns in managing technological change at scale. Moreover, the explicit mapping of established Operations Management principles like TPS (Andon, Kaizen, Jidoka, Genchi Gembutsu) to modern AI development tools demonstrates how foundational OM philosophies remain relevant and can guide the design of new, intelligent systems, leading to continuous improvement, reduced waste, and enhanced operational efficiency.

So this philosophy of continuous improvement through steady and consistent changes is something we embody at Toyota and are delighted to see with LangChain throughout the whole ecosystem.

**[13:04] Jidoka = LangGraph's human-in-the-loop design**
My personal favorite is the principle of Jidoka. Translated literally, it means automation with a human touch. And what LangGraph does really well is it automates a lot, or abstracts a lot of the nuance and monotony that, as an engineer, I don't want to have to deal with or don't care to deal with. But it keeps me in the loop. It keeps me plugged in so that I still have the values of a human and can still guide the product as it's being developed and deployed. And Jidoka is really like a handshake deal between AI, automation, and a human — to say: I understand each role that you play, and I understand we're going to have to adapt as technology progresses. On the manufacturing line, Jidoka means that a human understands we need automation in order to manufacture things very leanly and very efficiently. But as technology evolves, a human's role will change, because that automation will change. But a human is still critical to ensuring high quality of products and delivery to the final customer. So, yeah, LangGraph is a literal embodiment of the Jidoka principle, which we love.

**[14:11] Genchi Gembutsu = LangSmith trace-level debugging**
The next term is Genchi Gembutsu. This means to literally go to the source and understand what's going on. Try to find the root cause of the problem. We can't figure things out on a Teams call. If there's a manufacturing issue in Texas, we can't sit in California and try to figure out what's going on. The best way to solve the problem is to go to the manufacturing line, actually touch the hardware, understand the root cause of the problem, and then proliferate the solution throughout the rest of the manufacturing line so that we Kaizen the remedy. LangSmith traces are a direct embodiment of Genchi Gembutsu. For every query, every tool call, I can see the entire trace, the entire route to the solution. And if there's an issue, I can see exactly what caused it. And so it goes beyond the Andon board by giving me direct insight into what the problem is for any one of my products, my bots, my agents, and helps me as an engineer not have to sift through logs or spend a lot of time debugging. I can go directly to the problem, try to solve it, and keep our products up and running for our customers.

**[15:21] LangChain as the TPS for the AI era**
LangChain is a direct embodiment of the Toyota Production System philosophy. And while the framework is timeless, and TPS is the bedrock for all hardware manufacturing today, it hadn't been well translated into software or agentic manufacturing of agents — until LangChain. LangChain carries the legacy of TPS forward, allowing all these principles to be carried over into manufacturing for the new era of software engineering. And so I'm really excited to see where LangChain is going to go, because our cultures are so similar. And I fully believe that in the next few years — and actually probably now — the entire AI industry will look to LangChain as the TPS, or the LangChain Production System — the bedrock on which all the SaaS services, companies, et cetera, the entire industry is built on.
